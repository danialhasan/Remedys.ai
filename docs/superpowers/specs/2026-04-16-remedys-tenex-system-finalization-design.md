# Remedys Tenex-System Finalization Design

Date: 2026-04-16

## Purpose

Finalize `Remedys.ai` as a much closer structural and commercial analogue to `tenex.ai` while keeping the Remedys category, voice, and brand identity intact.

This is not a loose inspiration pass. The goal is to translate the current Remedys site from a premium brochure into a denser, more systemized, more commercial website that behaves like a full trust surface.

## Design Basis

The benchmark is Tenex in:

- page density
- section pacing
- visible desktop navigation
- repeated CTA rhythm
- immediate proof after the hero
- homepage offer layering
- lower-page authority and trust sections
- uniform service-page templates
- broader sitemap depth

The benchmark is not Tenex in:

- cybersecurity language
- enterprise-security visual tropes
- investor theater
- category-specific jargon

Remedys remains:

- practical AI consulting / education / implementation
- founder-led and operator-facing
- direct, concise, and intuitive in copy
- orange-accented rather than cyan-accented

## Resolved Product Decisions

The user explicitly approved the following:

- Remedys should be fairly similar to Tenex in most ways.
- The strongest top-of-site proof signal is founder/operator credibility.
- That founder/operator proof should appear immediately after the hero, not inside it.
- The header should move to a Tenex-style desktop nav with a visible CTA.
- The homepage should include a Tenex-like offer/package layer.
- The homepage should also include lower-page authority sections.
- The three service pages should become much more uniform as one system.
- The sitemap should expand beyond the current brochure footprint.
- Of the homepage top-third options, the preferred direction is the densest, most Tenex-like version.

## Goals

1. Increase commercial clarity in the first screen.
2. Make the site feel like one coherent operating system instead of a small set of attractive pages.
3. Establish founder/operator credibility as the primary trust anchor.
4. Make the three offers legible as structured ways to engage, not just adjacent service pages.
5. Build a fuller trust surface through additional pages and lower-page authority sections.
6. Preserve Remedys’s category fit and direct tone while borrowing Tenex’s structural confidence.

## Non-Goals

- Turning the site into a cybersecurity or enterprise software brand
- Adding fake proof, inflated metrics, or unsupported credibility claims
- Expanding into a large editorial program immediately
- Replacing the existing brand voice with jargon-heavy positioning

- `/insights/` becomes the authority surface, even if it initially launches with a small number of tightly written entries or structured insight cards.

## Global Site System

### Navigation

Replace the burger-first experience on desktop with a visible header system:

- logo at left
- visible desktop links
- persistent CTA at right
- burger or condensed behavior reserved for smaller breakpoints

Recommended desktop nav groups:

- Home
- Services
- Packages
- Why Remedys
- About
- Insights
- Contact
- CTA: `Get Started` or `Book Intro`

### Footer

The footer should become deeper and more useful, with grouped destinations that reinforce company depth:

- service links
- trust links
- company links
- contact path
- final CTA reinforcement

## Homepage Design

The homepage should be rebuilt as a sequence of commercial answers.

### Required Section Order

1. Visible desktop nav with CTA
2. Denser hero
3. Founder/operator credibility strip
4. Homepage offer/package layer
5. Human judgment vs system leverage section
6. Stronger process / how-it-works section
7. Founder/company authority block
8. Tool familiarity / workflow credibility block
9. Insights preview
10. Final CTA
11. Deep footer

### Hero

Keep:

- headline: `Put AI to work.`
- dark/orange palette
- technical hero-object language

Change:

- move the value explanation much closer to the headline
- reduce atmospheric emptiness
- make the CTA visible and immediate
- let the hero read as a business proposition first and an art direction moment second

The hero should feel closer to Tenex’s first-screen decisiveness, with more structure and less open silence.

### Founder/Operator Credibility Strip

This section sits immediately below the hero and acts as the first trust transfer.

Content type:

- founder-led
- operator-first
- built for SMBs and growing teams
- consulting to implementation

The design should use a compact proof-cell or stat-strip pattern rather than a soft paragraph block.

### Homepage Offer Layer

The homepage must include a Tenex-like engagement layer instead of forcing buyers to infer the offer structure.

Recommended content:

- Consulting
- Education
- Implementation

Each item should read like a buyable engagement shape, not just a category link. Hard pricing is optional; shape clarity is not.

### Human Judgment vs System Leverage

Replace flatter trust grids with a more polar, Tenex-like comparison:

- human judgment
- system speed / automation leverage

The point is to dramatize the model and make the value split legible.

### Process / How It Works

Keep the current practical orientation, but increase its visual authority.

Required changes:

- clearer step sequence
- stronger connectors or progression lines
- more visible visual continuity
- less soft spacing between cards or steps

### Lower-Page Authority

The homepage should feel like a company, not only a pitch.

Include:

- stronger founder/company block
- tool familiarity or workflow credibility section
- insights preview that routes into the authority surface

## Service-Page System

The service pages should no longer behave as three separate creative compositions. They should become one repeatable commercial template with service-specific copy and visuals.

### Shared Template

1. Strong hero with explicit service framing and immediate CTA
2. Quick proof / credibility strip
3. Practical “what this is” section
4. “Why teams need this” or breakdown section
5. “How we work” section
6. Outcomes / what you leave with section
7. Cross-links to other offers
8. Final CTA

### Uniform Elements

- hero layout
- section order
- CTA rhythm
- proof strip treatment
- process treatment
- footer CTA

### Variable Elements

- service promise
- visual motif
- specific examples
- service-specific risk framing
- CTA wording where appropriate

This creates the Tenex effect: the site reads as one operating model rather than three separately styled sales pages.

## New Supporting Pages

### `/services/`

Purpose:

- explain how the three offers fit together
- help buyers understand which path fits their stage
- connect the homepage to a stronger system page

### `/packages/`

Purpose:

- translate the three offers into clear engagement shapes
- give buyers a Tenex-like commercial orientation layer
- improve qualification before contact

This page should not require public pricing to be valuable. It should make scope, shape, and likely entry points obvious.

### `/why-remedys/`

Purpose:

- consolidate trust, differentiation, founder story, and operating model
- provide the “why trust you” destination that Tenex handles through deeper company/proof surfaces

### `/insights/`

Purpose:

- give the brand an authority surface
- hold practical content or structured insights related to AI operations, team enablement, and workflow adoption

This can launch lean. It only needs enough signal to make the site feel deeper and more current.

## Visual System

### Direction To Borrow From Tenex

- denser panels and section framing
- stronger separators
- more visible chips, proof cells, and structural markers
- more consistent section tension down the full page
- stronger repetition of CTA moments

### Direction To Keep As Remedys

- orange accent system
- practical AI category cues
- editorial display moment in the main headline
- founder-led tone rather than enterprise theater

### Spacing

The current site is too spacious for the target benchmark.

Required adjustment:

- compress vertical spacing
- reduce dead air between major blocks
- keep enough room for premium legibility, but bias toward commercial pacing

## Motion System

Keep the current hero motion and technical object language, but stop letting the hero be the only place where motion carries energy.

Add one or two recurring motion systems:

- proof-strip reveals
- section connector or process-line motion
- more deliberate hover energy on cards and CTA modules

Motion should reinforce progression and structure. It should not feel ornamental.

## Copy Direction

The copy should become more structured and more commercially decisive without becoming verbose.

Rules:

- short section intros
- faster explanation near headlines
- more obvious offer framing
- more repeated conversion cues
- more direct proof language tied to founder/operator credibility

Avoid:

- inflated AI rhetoric
- abstract mission language
- unsupported bold claims
- cyber-enterprise category language

## SEO / AEO / GEO Implications

The expanded sitemap creates better destination clarity for search, answer engines, and routing:

- `/services/` for umbrella intent
- `/packages/` for engagement-shape intent
- `/why-remedys/` for trust/differentiation intent
- `/insights/` for authority and topical coverage

Internal linking should make these pages part of the main user journey, not dead-end utilities.

## Risks And Guardrails

### Risks

- borrowing Tenex too literally and losing category fit
- adding density without clarity
- overbuilding authority sections before there is enough real content
- making the service pages too uniform to carry their distinct promises

### Guardrails

- Remedys must still read as practical AI consulting for SMBs and operators
- every proof cue must be credible today
- copy stays direct and concise
- visual density should increase, but each section still needs a clear job

## Definition Of Success

The redesign is successful if:

- the first screen explains the business faster
- the site feels much more commercially complete
- the homepage provides clear ways to engage without forcing visitors to infer the offer
- the service pages feel like one system
- the expanded sitemap creates deeper trust and authority without bloat
- the result feels recognizably closer to Tenex in structure and pacing while still clearly being Remedys
