# Remedys Client Brief Design Spec

Date: 2026-05-19
Status: awaiting user review
Owner: Shafan Khan
Asset: reusable external Remedys client-facing PDF

## Purpose

Create a reusable external PDF that helps prospective clients quickly understand Remedys, the problems it solves, how it works, how it fits with vendors and build teams, and what the next working session should accomplish.

The asset should be a consulting briefing with premium product-brochure polish. It should feel serious, specific, structured, and authored. It should not feel like a generic AI-generated deck.

Primary use cases:

- First-call follow-up.
- Warm referral context.
- Client education before a second call.
- Internal stakeholder forwarding.
- Buyer enablement when a prospect is comparing Remedys against a dev shop, BPO, internal team, outsourced vendor, or DIY AI tool.

## Positioning Source Of Truth

Use the current Remedys website context as the canonical positioning source:

- Core promise: `Put AI to work.`
- Primary audience: owners, operators, and internal business leaders.
- Positioning: Remedys helps businesses find the AI opportunities worth acting on, then build the workflows, training, and systems to make them real.
- Service paths:
  - `AI Consulting` / `Advisory through implementation.`
  - `AI Automation` / `Automate a workflow.`
  - `AI Enablement` / `Learn how to use AI well.`
  - `AI Engineering` / `Build something useful.`
- Process language:
  - `Discover the work`
  - `Map the opportunities`
  - `Choose the first move`
  - `Build the system`
  - `Drive adoption`

Avoid positioning Remedys as a strategy-only consultancy, a generic AI agency, a pure dev shop, or a prompt/tooling vendor.

## Approved Asset Direction

Use the Decision Briefing structure.

The asset should answer the buyer's questions in order:

1. What is Remedys?
2. Why does this matter now?
3. What does Remedys do?
4. How does Remedys work?
5. What are the routes into working together?
6. How does Remedys fit with dev shops, BPOs, vendors, and internal teams?
7. How fast does the work move?
8. What does the client receive?
9. Where does Squad fit?
10. What questions or objections are likely?
11. What should happen next?

## Format

- PDF.
- 12 pages.
- Landscape orientation preferred for readability and future slide reuse.
- Each page should carry one idea.
- Each page should be understandable in 30-45 seconds.
- Footer should include `Remedys` and page number.
- Avoid dense paragraphs and long consulting-document walls of text.

## Visual System

The visual system should match the current Remedys website direction:

- Sharp, technical, premium, direct, and human.
- Clean enough for business buyers.
- Distinct enough to avoid generic AI-site aesthetics.
- Restrained with strong hierarchy.

Canonical palette:

- Light paper base: `#f7f8fb`
- Dark ink: `#0b1020` / `#10121c`
- Violet action: `#7b4dff` / `#804bff`
- Ice-blue accent: `#72b7ff` / `#7bc4ff`
- Deep navy panels: `#070812`, `#112b4d`

Visual ingredients:

- thin grid
- subtle texture
- sharp rectangular panels
- minimal radius, around 4-8px
- sparse accent usage
- clear typographic hierarchy
- diagrams, process lines, tables, and artifact stacks

Avoid:

- beige editorial look
- generic purple-blue AI gradients
- abstract glowing orbs
- fake dashboards
- stock people
- generic AI avatars
- decorative icon grids
- bento grids that do not add information
- claims or visuals that imply proof that does not exist

Every visual must do one of four jobs:

1. Prove.
2. Explain.
3. Compare.
4. Orient.

If a visual only adds atmosphere, remove it.

## Typography And Hierarchy

Use a modern sans-serif system that feels like product documentation plus consulting clarity.

Hierarchy:

- Page title: large, direct, usually one line.
- Page takeaway: short supporting line, one to three lines maximum.
- Section labels: small uppercase or mono-style labels.
- Body copy: readable, generous line height, low density.
- Tables and process labels: compact and clear.

Cover exception:

- `Put AI to work.` should appear as a medium-weight signature line, not as a tiny tagline.

Avoid:

- huge heroic type on every page
- decorative typography
- tiny consulting footnote clutter
- long paragraphs
- text blocks that feel like exported Word pages

## Page Composition Rules

Use a consistent three-zone layout:

1. Top zone: page title and one-line takeaway.
2. Middle zone: diagram, table, process, or structured explanation.
3. Bottom zone: footer and page number.

Each page needs one main object:

- movement table
- comparison
- service lanes
- process line
- engagement depth map
- vendor route matrix
- cadence timeline
- deliverable stack
- Squad delivery loop
- FAQ grid
- working-session map

Use cards only for repeated items. Do not nest cards inside cards.

Title:

`You do not have to choose between speed and discipline.`

### Page 11: FAQ

Title:

`FAQ`

Questions:

`Can Remedys work with our existing vendor, BPO, dev shop, or internal team?`

`Yes. We can advise, govern, review, or support the work without owning the entire build.`

`Why not just use a dev shop?`

`A dev shop can be useful for execution. The risk is that they may build what is requested without fully resolving workflow, product, AI behavior, data, evals, adoption, or maintainability.`

`Can you help us evaluate a vendor quote or proposal?`

`Yes. We can review scope, assumptions, architecture, delivery plan, risks, acceptance criteria, and missing pieces before you commit.`

`Can you build the product end-to-end?`

`Yes, when the scope, timeline, and ownership model are clear.`

`What if we already have a prototype?`

`That is often a strong starting point. We review what exists, identify what is missing, and define what it would take to make it useful, reviewable, and maintainable.`

`Do you only do AI strategy?`

`No. Remedys is advisory through implementation. We help with discovery, specs, build, governance, rollout, adoption, and iteration.`

`How fast can we get started?`

`Usually with a focused working session and a clear material request. For bounded prototype, vendor, or workflow reviews, the first useful read can often happen within days once materials are shared.`

`How do you price work?`

`Pricing depends on scope, risk, timeline, technical complexity, and the level of involvement needed. After discovery, we recommend the right path.`

`What do you need from us?`

`Business context, workflow examples, current tools, prototype or vendor materials if available, decision timeline, budget range, and the people who will own the work.`

Main visual:

Two-column FAQ grid. Keep answers short.

Buyer belief:

`They anticipated our concerns.`

## Discovery Logic Underneath Page 12

Do not name the frameworks in the PDF. Use their logic underneath.

The working-session questions cover:

- SPIN: situation, pain, implication, need-payoff.
- SPICED: situation, pain, impact, critical event, decision.
- MEDDICC / MEDDPICC: metrics, economic buyer, decision criteria, decision process, pain, champion, competition, paper process.
- BANT: budget, authority, need, timing.
- Consulting discovery: current state, future state, gap, constraints, risks, success criteria.
- Product discovery: users, jobs to be done, workflows, edge cases, acceptance criteria.
- Technical discovery: systems, data, integrations, security, QA, observability, handoff.

The client-facing page should feel like a structured working session, not a sales qualification script.

## Anti-Slop Rules

The asset must not include:

- generic `AI transformation` language
- `unlock`, `supercharge`, `revolutionize`, or `seamless`
- stock people
- AI-generated people or fake customer environments
- fake logos
- fake testimonials
- unsupported metrics
- decorative orb or abstract AI hero imagery
- decorative icon rows
- fake dashboards
- overdesigned bento-grid pages
- claims that Remedys guarantees ROI, compliance, fully autonomous output, or perfect production readiness

The asset should include:

- specific operating language
- clear engagement paths
- tangible deliverables
- honest timing windows
- vendor/build-team optionality
- Squad as governed delivery discipline
- a practical next-step working session

## Quality Bar

The finished PDF should score highly against these criteria:

- A busy buyer can understand the offer in under 60 seconds.
- The story flows from context to decision to next step.
- Each page answers one question.
- The service paths match the website source of truth.
- The copy is plain, specific, and low-fluff.
- The design feels authored and intentional.
- The visual hierarchy makes the reading path obvious.
- The buyer understands there are multiple routes into working with Remedys.
- The asset respectfully positions Remedys against dev shops, BPOs, outsourced teams, internal teams, and DIY AI tools.
- The FAQ reduces real buyer anxiety.
- The next working session is framed as useful and structured.

- all text fits on page

- page titles tell the story on their own
- cover uses `Put AI to work.` at medium weight
- color usage matches Remedys palette
- no generic AI imagery is introduced
- PDF is readable when printed or forwarded
- links and contact details are correct if included
